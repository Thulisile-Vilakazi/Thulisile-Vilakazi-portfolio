About

Before pursuing software, I studied law at the University of Cape Town — where precision, structure, and clear communication are non-negotiable. That mindset now shapes how I design systems, debug issues, and collaborate: start with facts, clarify constraints, then build logic that stands up under pressure.

At WeThinkCode_, I’ve delivered projects that mirror real industry work: a Python ETL Bus Schedule App that standardised public transport data, a Django Legal Companion turning customary marriage law into step-by-step guidance, and a Java Client-Server “Robot Worlds” simulation with unit tests and CI/CD. These taught me to move from concept to implementation in collaborative, Agile environments.

I’m comfortable in Python, Java, JavaScript, and SQL. I value clean architecture, readable code, and documentation that future teammates can rely on. I’m actively looking for an internship or junior role where I can contribute quickly, learn from experienced engineers, and keep building software that makes complex things usable.

My Approach

Blend a lawyer’s attention to detail with a developer’s curiosity. I prioritise maintainability, tests, and clear naming — so solutions are robust today and easier to extend tomorrow.

How I Collaborate

I thrive in teams that share knowledge and feedback. I keep communication clear, commits tidy, and tickets moving — and I document decisions so the team can move faster together.

What I’m After

A junior engineering role where I can contribute to meaningful products in data, fintech, or civic tech, grow under mentorship, and keep shipping thoughtful, user-centred software.
